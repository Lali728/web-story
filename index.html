Why Applications Fail

  • Ineligible Loan Types: Applying for a plan like IBR with a Parent PLUS loan.
  • Application Errors: Simple typos, missing signatures, or incorrect income docs.
  • Default Status: You must cure a default before applying for a new plan.

The Current Crisis

In mid-2024, the affordable SAVE Plan was invalidated by courts, halting millions of applications and trapping borrowers in administrative forbearance without the interest subsidy.

How to Avoid Denial

  • Double-check your application for errors.
  • Use the IRS Data Retrieval Tool on StudentAid.gov.
  • Confirm your loan types are eligible for the plan you want.
  • Never miss your annual income recertification deadline.

Avenues for Recourse

If denied, don't just give up. Your first step is to contact your loan servicer to understand the exact reason.

For systemic issues, you can escalate your case to the FSA Ombudsman Group or the CFPB.

Quick FAQs

Does a denial affect my credit?
Not directly, but missing payments because of it will.

Are private loans eligible?
No, these plans are for federal loans only. Contact your private lender for options.
